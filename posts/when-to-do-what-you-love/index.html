    When to Do What You Love

    何时去做你热爱的事
    June 8, 2025

      这是一篇译文，原文作者是Paul Graham。 大一的时候就拜读过阮一峰老师翻译的Paul Graham文集<黑客与画家>，并深受影响。

      There’s some debate about whether it’s a good idea to “follow your passion”. In fact the question is impossible to answer with a simple yes or no. Sometimes you should and sometimes you shouldn’t, but the border between should and shouldn’t is very complicated. The only way to give a general answer is to trace it.

      「从事你真正热爱的事业」是否是个好主意，长久以来都争论不断。事实上，这个问题很难用一个简单的「是」或「否」来回答。有时候你应该而有时候你不应该，而要搞清这两者的边界非常复杂。

      （“follow your passion"直译为「追随你的热情」，在英文语境下经常用于激励人们找到自己真正热爱的事情，并将其作为事业或生活的方向。这种表达在汉语文化里比较罕见，很少有父母或老师对自己的孩子和学生说「追随你的热情吧」，他们会说，好好读书，将来才能出人头地。 所谓「出人头地」，就是大多数国人心目中的成功。至于你内心是否充盈是否快乐，不会有人在意（甚至也会被自己内心刻意忽略）。这里我没有用「追随你的热情」这种在中文语境里略显抽象的表达，而是「从事你真正热爱的事业」这种稍微具象一点的表达。）

      When people talk about this question, there’s always an implicit “instead of”. All other things being equal, why shouldn’t you work on what interests you the most? So even raising the question implies that all other things aren’t equal, and that you have to choose between working on what interests you the most and something else, like what pays the best.

      当人们说起「从事你真正热爱的事业」时，后面往往还隐含着「而不是…」的表达。如果所有这些事都是等同的，那么没道理不去做你最热爱的事。
      所以，让我们现实一点，你必须在你最热爱的事和其他事 —— 比如报酬最多的事 —— 之间做出选择。

      And indeed if your main goal is to make money, you can’t usually afford to work on what interests you the most. People pay you for doing what they want, not what you want. But there’s an obvious exception: when you both want the same thing. For example, if you love football, and you’re good enough at it, you can get paid a lot to play it.

      如果你的主要目标是赚钱，你通常都做不了真正感兴趣的事。老板付钱让你做他们想做的事，而不是你想做的事。有一个例外：你和付你钱的人想做的是同一件事。比如，如果你喜欢足球，并且踢得足够好，那么你可以通过踢球赚到很多钱（就像梅西和C罗一样）。

      Of course the odds are against you in a case like football, because so many other people like playing it too. This is not to say you shouldn’t try though. It depends how much ability you have and how hard you’re willing to work.

      不过在足球这个例子上你胜算并不大，因为世界上有太多喜欢踢球的人了。不是说你不该勇于尝试，这主要取决于你的能力以及你愿意为之付出多大努力。

      The odds are better when you have strange tastes: when you like something that pays well and that few other people like. For example, it’s clear that Bill Gates truly loved running a software company. He didn’t just love programming, which a lot of people do. He loved writing sofeware for customers. That is a very strange taste indeed, but if you have it, you can make a lot by indulging it.

      如果你品味独特：喜欢的是报酬可观并且少有人喜欢的事，胜算会稍微大一点。举个例子，Bill Gates肯定真的热爱经营一家软件公司。他不止热爱编程（很多人都热爱编程），他还热爱为满足顾客需求写软件。这品味的确独特，如果你投入其中也确实可以挣到很多钱。

      There are even some people who have a genuine intellectual interest in making money. This is distinct from mere greed. They just can’t help noticing when something is mispriced, and can’t help doing something about it. It’s like a puzzle for them.

      甚至有一些人天生就对赚钱感兴趣。这不同于纯粹的贪婪。当他们注意到有事物被市场错误定价时，他们就会忍不住做一些事（去纠错）。这个过程对他们来说就像解决一道谜题一样有趣。

      In fact there’s an edge case here so spectacular that it turns all the preceding advice on its head. If you want to make a really huge amount of money – hundrds of millions or even billions of dollars – it turns out to be very useful to work on what interests you the most. The reason is not the extra motivation you get from doing this, but that the way to make a really large amount of money is to start a startup, and working on what interests you is an excellent way to discover startup ideas.

      有一个特例，它是如此惊人以至于足以推翻所有上面的建议。如果你真的想挣一笔大钱 —— 百万千万甚至上亿，事情证明，做你最感兴趣的事往往是最有效的。理由不是你有额外的动力（兴趣）去做这件事，而是开公司创业本身就是挣大钱的方式，而做让你感兴趣的事，是发现创业点子的绝佳的方式。

      Many if not most of the biggest startups began as projects the founders were doing for fun. Apple, Google, and Facebook all began that way. Why is this pattern so common? Because the best ideas tend to be such outliers that you’d overlook them if you were consciously looking for ways to make money. Whereas if you’re young and good at technology, your unconscious instincts about what would be interesting to work on are very well aligned with what needs to be built.

      虽不能说绝大多数，但确实有很多日后成为大公司的企业，始于几位创始人出于兴趣而开展的项目。苹果、Google以及Facebook都是这样的公司。为什么这一模式如此相近，因为最棒的点子通常过于“不走寻常路”(outliers)，如果是抱着赚钱的心态，那么大概率你会忽略这些点子。然而你如果很年轻并且擅长技术，你潜意识里感兴趣的事，往往和这个世界真正缺少、值得建设的东西不谋而合。

      So there’s something like a midwit peak for making money. If you don’t need to make much, you can work on whatever you’re most interested in; If you want to become moderately rich, you can’t usually afford to; but if you want to become super rich, and you’re yound and good at technology, working on what you’re most interested in becomes a good idea again.

      在「挣钱」这件事情上存在一些反直觉的观点。如果你不需要挣很多钱，你可以做任何你感兴趣的事；如果你想挣得中等收入，你通常不能做你感兴趣的事；但如果你想挣非常多的钱，并且你年轻又擅长技术，做你感兴趣的事就又成为了一个好选项。

      （midwit —— 直译为中等智力，指聪明但不够聪明到看穿问题本质的人；midwit peak —— 指对问题误解最深最自信的阶段）

      What if you’re not sure what you want? What if you’re attracted to the idea of making money and more attracted to some kinds of work than others, but neither attraction predominates? How do you break ties?

      但假如你不确定你内心真正想要什么呢？假如你既想挣钱，也想做你喜欢的事，但两者都不足以主导你做出抉择，这时应该如何打破僵局呢。

      The key here is to understand that such ties are only apparent. When you have trouble choosing between following your interests and makding money, it’s never because you have complete knowledge of yourself and of the types of work you’re choosing between, and the options are perfectly balanced.

      问题的核心在于，你得明白僵局只是表象。当你难于在挣钱和从事感兴趣的事业之间做出抉择时，从来不是因为这两个选项势均力敌，而是你对自己、对事业都还不够了解。

      （Graham这里的潜台词是要通过行动获得认知、完善认知，而不是只靠想象做判断）

      When you can’t decide which path to take, it’s almost always due to ignorance. In fact you’re usually suffering from three kinds of ignorance simultaneously: you don’t know what makes you happy, what the various kinds of work are really like, or how well you could do them.

      如果你不能决定走哪条路，原因几乎总是可以归结为「无知」。实际上，你很可能一直在同时承受三种「无知」：

      • 什么使你快乐
      • 不同的工作究竟是什么样子
      • 以及对于每样工作你究竟有多擅长。

      （最理想的情况是，能使你感到快乐的工作正是你最感兴趣的事情，并且也是你最擅长的事情。）

      In a way this ignorance is excusable. it’s ofen hard to predict these things, and no one even tells you that you need to. If you’re ambitious you’re told you should go to college, and this is good advice so far as it goes, but that’s where it usually ends. No one tells you how to figure out what to work on, or how hard this can be.

      某种程度上这种「无知」是可以被原谅的。因为预知这些很难，没人能告诉你应该做什么。如果你有雄心壮志，你应该去读大学，这一直是一个好的建议。但人生建议通常就止于此了，没人能告诉你读完大学之后之后应该做什么，以及弄明白做什么这件事本身有多难。

      What do you do in the face of uncertainty? Get more certainty. And probably the best way to do that is to try working on things you’re interested in. That will get you more information about how interested you are in them.

      如何应对不确定性 —— 获得更多确定性。也许获得确定性最好的方式就是从事你感兴趣的事业，因为在这个过程中，你会更加了解你究竟对此有多少兴趣。

      Don’t wait . Don’t wait till the end of college to figure out what to work on. Don’t even wait for internships during college. You don’t necessarily need a job doing x in order to work on x; ofen you can just start doing it in some form yourself. And since figuring out what to work on is a problem that could take years to solve, the sooner you start, the better.

      别坐以待毙。别等到大学毕业才开始思考从事什么事业，甚至不要等待实习机会，你不必因为想做某件事而找一份和这件事相关的工作，通常你都可以自己开始。 鉴于可能要花上多年时间才能弄明白从事什么事业，所以越早开始越好。

      One useful trick for judging different kinds of work is to look at who your colleagues will be. You’ll become like whoever you work with. Do you want to become like these people?

      评判不同工作的一个管用的方法是观察你未来的同事。你最终会被你周围的同事所同化。所以，不妨问问自己，你想成为他们那样的人吗。

      Indeed, the difference in character between different kinds of work is magnified by the fact that everyone else is facing the same decisions as you. If you choose a kind of work mainly for how well it pays, you’ll be surrounded by other people who chose it for the same reason, and that will make it even more soul-sucking than it seems from the outside. Whereas if you choose work you’re genuinely interested in, you’ll be surrounded mostly by other people who are genuinely interested in it, and that will make it extra inspiring.

      不同的工作之间差异非常巨大。因为其他人会面临和你一样的抉择（丰厚的收入还是追随热爱）。如果你出于报酬丰厚而选择这份工作，你会被出于同样理由选择这份工作的人所包围（正如我的上一份工作），作为一个外人来看，这一点会让这份工作更加恶心。然而如果你是出于真正的兴趣而选择这份工作，你会被同样出于兴趣而选择这份工作的人包围，这会是这份工作额外的激励。

      （恶心的原文用词是soul-sucking，其实非常贴切，那样一份工作会不断吸噬你的灵魂，把你掏空，最终你会变成一具行尸走肉，对生活毫无热情，我之前的同事们很多都接近这种状态。）

      The other thing you do in the face of uncertainty is to make choice that are uncertainty-proof. The less sure you are about what to do, the more important it is to choose options that give you more options in the future. I call this “staying upwind”. If you’re unsure whether to major in math or economics, for example, choose math; math is upwind of economics in the sense that it will be easier to switch later from math to economics than from economics to math.

      面对不确定性时的另一个选择是「防守」。你越不确定现在要做什么，选择一个能在将来给你更多选择的事情就越重要。我称之为「待在上风口」，举个例子，如果你不确定应该主修经济还是主修数学，你应该选择数学，数学就是经济的上风口，因为如果你之后想从数学转为经济，会比反过来更容易。

      （玩过<荒野的召唤>、<荒野大镖客>或者诸如此类包含狩猎元素的游戏的玩家应该对「上风口」这个概念不陌生，待在上风口，意味着你的气味不会被吹向猎物，否则就会惊动猎物，失去绝佳的狩猎机会。）

      （从二十岁到三十岁，我时常会感慨路仿佛越走越窄了，可以做出的选择越来越少了。虽然别人会告诉你，人生是旷野，不是轨道。）

      There’s one case, though, where it’s easy to say whether you should work on what interests you the most: If you want to do great work. This is not a sufficient condition for doing great work, but it is a necessary one.

      在一种情形下，很容易回答「你是否应该做你最感兴趣的事」这个问题。那就是，你想做great work，这时从事你最感兴趣的事业就成了必要条件，而非充分条件。

      （这里Graham所说的great work和Jobs说的是一件事），即在某个领域取得一些成就，而非仅为了养家糊口而做的ordinary work。

      There’s a lot of selection bias in advice about whether to “follow your passion”, and this is the reason. Most such advice comes from people who are famously successful, and if you ask someone who’s famously successful how to do what they did, most will tell you that you have to work on what you’re most interested in. And this is in fact true.

      其实对于是否要选择从事你感兴趣的事业这个问题上的建议存在很多「选择偏见」，因为这些建议通常都来自功成名就的人，如果你去问这样的人，应该如何像他们一样成功，大多数的回答都是：去做你最感兴趣的事。对他们来说确实如此。

      That doesn’t mean it’s the right advice for everyone. Not everyone can do great work, or wants to. But if you do want to, the complicated question of whether or not wo work on what interests you the most becomes simple. The answer is yes. The root of great work is a sort of ambitious curiosity, and you can’t manufacture that.

      但这个建议并不适用所有人。不是所有人都能做或者想做great work。但如果你想做great work，是否要做你最感兴趣的事情这个问题就变得极其简单了。答案就是"yes”。做great work的根源来自ambitious curiosity，这股动力是无法遏止和假装的。

      （ambitious curiosity —— 直译为有抱负的好奇心，比较抽象，Graham大概是想和普通的好奇心，或者说求知欲有所区分。光有好奇心/求知欲还不够，还需要有一定的目标感甚至使命感，才有可能在一个领域 —— 你最感兴趣的领域 —— 取得成就。）

      愿我们都能从事自己热爱的事业。

      （End）
